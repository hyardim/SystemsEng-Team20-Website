Central overview

Timeline at a glance

The project moved through five practical phases: early discovery and requirement definition, original server setup, infrastructure pivot after access loss, feature completion and stabilization, then an extended final testing period.

The cards below summarise each milestone pair of weeks. Use the left timeline or the week titles to open the detailed page for that stage of the project.

2026 entries

Week 23–24

2026 · Testing wave 3, evidence packaging, and handover readiness

We focused on documentation, handover, and a final regression pass so the system could be demonstrated cleanly without extra setup.

Week 21–22

2026 · Testing wave 2: performance, reliability, and usability

These weeks were about speed and reliability: measuring latency, tuning retrieval and routing, and improving failure behaviour.

Week 19–20

2026 · Testing wave 1: functional and integration coverage

We ran end-to-end tests across the GP-to-specialist workflow and used the issues we found to drive a fix-and-retest cycle.

Week 17–18

2026 · Local-first routing and cloud fallback

This is when the routing setup became usable: local Med42 by default, cloud fallback for harder queries, and continued work on citations and grounding.

Week 15–16

2026 · Local Med42 and RunPod deployment plan

We settled on the local 8B version of Med42 for routine use and chose RunPod as the current cloud option for heavier inference.

Week 13–14

2026 · Intel server loss and the routing pivot

Losing the Gaudi server forced a backend rethink, which led to the routing-layer plan and a first fully working RAG pipeline that still needed optimisation.

Week 11–12

2026 · Merging the GP flow and chatbot

Client feedback led us to merge the GP query flow and chatbot into one connected experience, while RAG work and frontend tweaks continued.

2025 entries

Week 9–10

2025 · Implementation begins with RAG ingestion

Implementation properly started here, with RAG ingestion, chunking strategy, early database setup, and a first frontend prototype.

Week 7–8

2025 · Gaudi 2 stabilisation and safety refinements

We stabilised the Gaudi 2 setup, documented the environment, and kept tightening citation mapping and specialist review behaviour.

Week 5–6

2025 · Gaudi 2 setup and Med42 70B bring-up

A lot of time went into getting the Gaudi 2 environment and Med42 70B running reliably, because the infrastructure shaped the rest of the project.

Week 3–4

2025 · Requirements baseline and Gaudi planning

We turned the interview findings into prioritised requirements, identified the main risks, and planned the initial Gaudi 2 deployment path.

Week 1–2

2025 · Problem framing and specialist interviews

Specialist interviews helped us define the problem, narrow the scope to neurology and rheumatology, and set the baseline around evidence and oversight.
