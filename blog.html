Central overview

Timeline at a glance

The project moved through five practical phases: early discovery and requirement definition, original server setup, infrastructure pivot after access loss, feature completion and stabilization, then an extended final testing period.

The cards below summarise each milestone pair of weeks. Use the left timeline or the week titles to open the detailed page for that stage of the project.

2026 entries

Week 23–24

2026 · Testing wave 3, evidence packaging, and handover readiness

The final weeks consolidated regression evidence, tightened reporting consistency across the site, and prepared a clearer handover story aligned with the implemented system.

Week 21–22

2026 · Testing wave 2: performance, reliability, and usability

The second testing wave concentrated on non-functional behaviour, repeatedly re-running critical scenarios after each batch of improvements to keep regressions under control.

Week 19–20

2026 · Testing wave 1: functional and integration coverage

The final phase opened with deliberate end-to-end testing across GP, specialist, and admin workflows, feeding directly into a fix-and-retest loop.

Week 17–18

2026 · Integration hardening and defect burn-down

Before final validation, effort concentrated on review-state edge cases, retrieval and fallback reliability, and keeping the containerised setup reproducible.

Week 15–16

2026 · Platform breadth beyond core answering

The system expanded beyond question answering into a more usable platform, with structured submission flows, attachments, notifications, and admin-facing visibility.

Week 13–14

2026 · Workflow simplification and specialist gate hardening

Requirement refinement led to a cleaner product decision: merge assistant-like interactions into a single consultation-based path and make review state changes more traceable and auditable.

Week 11–12

2026 · Local-first route operational

After the pivot, the team restored a stable local-first path and validated failure handling so infrastructure changes did not alter the user-facing consultation and review journey.

2025 entries

Week 9–10

2025 · Server access lost and the architecture pivot begins

Losing access to the original server forced the biggest pivot in the project: the clinical workflow had to stay intact while the deployment path moved toward local processing with controlled cloud fallback.

Week 7–8

2025 · Stabilization while still on the original server

Once the first live runs worked, the focus shifted to making the setup repeatable, documenting it properly, and tightening the system behaviour around citations and specialist review.

Week 5–6

2025 · Original server setup and first integrations

A large share of the work went into getting the original Gaudi 2 environment and Med42 70B setup running reliably, because infrastructure constraints directly shaped what the system could realistically deliver.

Week 3–4

2025 · Requirement baseline and acceptance criteria

Interview findings became explicit functional and non-functional requirements, prioritised with MoSCoW, alongside measurable acceptance criteria for latency, auditability, and reliability.

Week 1–2

2025 · Problem framing and first specialist interviews

We began by mapping Advice and Guidance pain points with specialists and identifying the minimum conditions for a safe clinical support workflow: evidence visibility, specialist accountability, and a constrained domain.
