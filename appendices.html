User manual

Ambience-AI-1.5 is a role-aware clinical consultation platform. GPs create consultations and receive AI-generated answers grounded in ingested medical guidelines. Specialists review and validate those answers before they reach clinical use. Admins oversee the platform, manage users, and upload new evidence documents. This manual covers each role end-to-end.

System overview

The application is fully containerised. Running docker compose up --build starts all seven services and seeds three demo accounts automatically. Open http://localhost:3000 in any modern browser to begin.

  • GPs create consultations, ask clinical questions, and receive grounded AI answers with numbered citations drawn from ingested clinical guidelines.
  • Specialists review AI responses, inspect the cited evidence, and decide to approve, reject, request changes, or write a manual answer.
  • Admins monitor platform-wide statistics, manage user accounts, review all consultations, trace audit logs, and upload new PDF clinical documents for RAG ingestion.

New self-registered accounts require email verification. In local development, verification and password-reset emails are delivered to Mailpit at http://localhost:8025. The demo accounts below are pre-verified and do not require this step.

Authentication

Navigate to http://localhost:3000 and use the credentials above to log in without any extra steps. If you register a new account instead, a verification link is sent to the email you provide. Open Mailpit at localhost:8025, find the message, and click the link — it expires after 60 minutes. A Resend verification option appears on the login screen if the link expires. The Forgot password flow follows the same mechanism: check Mailpit for the reset link, which expires after 30 minutes.

Role 1 — GP

GP workflow

GPs manage consultations from creation through to receiving a specialist review outcome.

  • Log in → the dashboard lists all your consultations with status badges.
  • Click New Consultation. Enter a title, select a specialty, and set a severity level (routine, urgent, emergency).
  • Inside the consultation, type a clinical question and submit. The system routes it through the RAG pipeline: vector search + keyword search + reranking produce a grounded answer with numbered citations below each response.
  • Attach supporting files (PDF, images) via the upload button within the chat. Uploaded files are stored and visible in the consultation timeline.
  • Use the filter controls to narrow your consultation list by status, specialty, or date range.
  • When a specialist claims the case, its status updates to In review. Once the specialist acts, the outcome (approved, rejected, changes requested, or manual response) appears in the timeline and you receive an in-app notification.
  • If changes are requested, revise the clinical context or question and resubmit. The updated consultation re-enters the review queue.
Role 2 — Specialist

Specialist workflow

Specialists validate AI-generated answers against the cited evidence before they are visible to GPs as confirmed outcomes.

  • Log in → the review queue lists all consultations awaiting specialist attention across all GPs.
  • Open a case and click Assign to me to claim it and remove it from the shared queue.
  • Read the full AI answer and expand each numbered citation to inspect the source passage from the ingested guidelines.
  • Choose one of four review outcomes:
    • Approve — AI answer is clinically sound; outcome released to GP.
    • Reject — AI answer is not acceptable; GP is notified.
    • Request changes — returns the consultation to the GP with notes explaining what to clarify.
    • Manual response — replace the AI answer with a specialist-written response.
  • Add review notes to any decision to document the clinical reasoning.
  • Close the consultation once review is complete. The GP receives an in-app notification with the outcome.
Role 3 — Admin

Admin workflow

Admins have platform-wide visibility for governance, user management, and evidence ingestion.

  • Log in → the admin dashboard shows platform statistics: total consultations, registered users, AI request counts, and review throughput.
  • Users — inspect all accounts, their roles, email verification status, and activation state. Admins can deactivate accounts or change role assignments.
  • Chats — view all consultations across all GPs for operational oversight.
  • Audit logs — trace security-relevant events including login attempts, role changes, and admin actions, with timestamps and actor IDs.
  • Guidelines — upload new PDF clinical documents. The RAG background worker (rag_worker service) automatically extracts, chunks, embeds with all-MiniLM-L6-v2, and stores vectors in PostgreSQL with pgvector. Uploaded documents immediately extend the evidence base for new consultations.
AI citations and grounding

Every AI answer lists numbered citations from ingested clinical guidelines. The RAG service uses hybrid vector + keyword search and cross-encoder reranking for high relevance. Specialists see the exact source passage next to each citation number.

In-app notifications

Role-appropriate notifications appear for all key workflow events: consultation status changes, specialist assignments, review outcomes (approve, reject, changes requested), and admin actions. Notifications are dismissed individually from the notification panel.

Profile management

All users can update their display name and change their password from the profile screen. Admins can also update role assignments and account activation states from the Users panel without needing direct database access.

Deployment manual

Ambience-AI-1.5 runs as seven Docker containers orchestrated with Docker Compose. A single command builds and starts the full stack. This manual covers local development setup, required environment variables, LLM configuration, and the steps needed for a production TLS deployment.

Prerequisites

  • Docker Desktop — required (Mac, Windows, or Linux). Minimum version 24.x recommended.
  • 8 GB RAM available to Docker — needed to run all seven containers comfortably.
  • Git — to clone the repository.
  • Ollama (optional) — for local LLM inference. Install from ollama.com, then pull the model: ollama pull thewindmom/llama3-med42-8b. No GPU is required but generation is significantly faster with one.

No Python or Node.js installation is needed on the host machine. All build and runtime dependencies are resolved inside Docker images at build time.

Quick start — local development

Step 1 — Clone the repository

terminal
git clone https://github.com/hyardim/Ambience-AI-1.5.git
cd Ambience-AI-1.5

Step 2 — Create environment files

Copy the provided example files to create your own .env files. At minimum, set the three required variables shown below.

terminal
cp backend/.env.example backend/.env
cp rag_service/.env.example rag_service/.env
backend/.env — required values
SECRET_KEY=your-secret-key-here
POSTGRES_PASSWORD=your-db-password-here
RAG_INTERNAL_API_KEY=your-rag-api-key-here

# Enable demo accounts (gp@, specialist@, admin@ / password123)
AUTH_BOOTSTRAP_DEMO_USERS=true
rag_service/.env — required values
POSTGRES_PASSWORD=your-db-password-here
RAG_INTERNAL_API_KEY=your-rag-api-key-here

Step 3 — Build and start

terminal
docker compose up --build

First build takes 5–10 minutes as Docker downloads base images and installs dependencies. Subsequent starts without --build are much faster. Wait until you see the frontend service report ready in the compose log before opening the browser.

Step 4 — Open the application

Navigate to http://localhost:3000. Log in with one of the demo accounts: gp@example.com, specialist@example.com, or admin@example.com — all with password password123. Demo accounts are only created when AUTH_BOOTSTRAP_DEMO_USERS=true.

Service stack

mailpit

Local SMTP server and email UI. Captures all outgoing emails (verification, password reset) without sending to real inboxes. UI at :8025, SMTP on :1025.

redis

Redis 7 Alpine. Used for RAG background job queuing (rq), API response caching, and rate-limit counters for auth endpoints. Capped at 256 MB with LRU eviction.

db_vector

PostgreSQL 16 with pgvector extension. Stores all application data and RAG chunk embeddings. SQL migrations and a compressed seed of RAG chunks are auto-applied on first start via docker-entrypoint-initdb.d.

backend

FastAPI service on port 8000. Handles authentication (JWT + refresh tokens), consultation lifecycle, notifications, audit logging, and role-enforced access control. Depends on db_vector and redis.

rag_service

FastAPI RAG engine on port 8001. Performs ingestion, hybrid retrieval, cross-encoder reranking, relevance scoring, and LLM routing (local Ollama or cloud RunPod). Exposes an internal API protected by RAG_INTERNAL_API_KEY.

rag_worker

Background RQ worker that processes document ingestion jobs queued by the RAG service. Extracts, chunks, embeds, and stores vectors in PostgreSQL asynchronously without blocking API requests.

frontend

React + Vite application on port 3000. Serves the GP, specialist, and admin interfaces with hot-module reloading in development. In production the nginx container handles TLS termination and proxying.

Environment variables

Required variables

The three variables below must be set before the stack will start. The compose file uses :? notation so Docker will refuse to start with a clear error if any are missing.

backend/.env and rag_service/.env
SECRET_KEY            # JWT signing secret — use a long random string (64+ chars)
POSTGRES_PASSWORD     # PostgreSQL password for the 'admin' user
RAG_INTERNAL_API_KEY  # Shared secret between backend and rag_service — any string

LLM routing

The RAG service can use a local Ollama instance or a cloud-hosted RunPod endpoint. The routing threshold controls when complex queries are escalated to the cloud model.

rag_service/.env — LLM settings
# Local Ollama (default, optional)
OLLAMA_BASE_URL=http://host.docker.internal:11434
OLLAMA_MODEL=thewindmom/llama3-med42-8b
LOCAL_LLM_TIMEOUT_SECONDS=120

# Cloud RunPod endpoint (optional — leave blank to use local only)
CLOUD_LLM_BASE_URL=https://api.runpod.ai/v2/YOUR_ENDPOINT/openai
CLOUD_LLM_MODEL=m42-health/Llama3-Med42-70B
CLOUD_LLM_API_KEY=your-runpod-api-key

# Score threshold 0–1: queries below this route to local, above to cloud
LLM_ROUTE_THRESHOLD=0.65

If neither Ollama nor a cloud key is configured, the RAG service will still start but LLM generation calls will fail. Retrieval, chunking, and embedding still work independently.

Demo accounts and email

backend/.env — auth and email settings
# Seed demo GP, specialist, and admin accounts on startup
AUTH_BOOTSTRAP_DEMO_USERS=true

Production deployment

The stack includes an nginx container with TLS support that is disabled by default (it belongs to the production Docker Compose profile). To enable it, set real SMTP credentials, place SSL certificates in nginx/certs/, and start with the production profile:

terminal — production start
# Set your domain in backend/.env
FRONTEND_BASE_URL=https://your-domain.example.com
COOKIE_SECURE=true

# Start with nginx TLS termination
docker compose --profile production up --build -d

When NGINX_GENERATE_SELF_SIGNED=true (the default), nginx generates a self-signed certificate on startup — useful for staging. Replace with a real cert from Let's Encrypt or another CA for production. Place cert.pem and key.pem in nginx/certs/.

Stopping and cleanup

terminal
# Stop all containers (preserve database volumes)
docker compose down

# Stop and remove all volumes including the database
docker compose down -v

# Rebuild from scratch (e.g. after changing dependencies)
docker compose build --no-cache
docker compose up

Use docker compose down -v with caution — it permanently deletes the PostgreSQL data volume including all consultations and ingested RAG chunks.

Troubleshooting

Services fail to start

Check that Docker Desktop is running and that no other process is occupying ports 3000, 8000, 8001, 8025, or 5432. Run docker compose ps to see which containers exited and docker compose logs <service> for detailed output.

Demo login fails

Ensure AUTH_BOOTSTRAP_DEMO_USERS=true is set in backend/.env before the first start. If the backend started without it, run docker compose restart backend after adding the variable. Demo accounts are created during startup only.

No verification email received

Verification emails in local dev are delivered to Mailpit, not a real inbox. Open http://localhost:8025 to find the message. If the mailpit container is not running, check docker compose logs mailpit.

AI response hangs or times out

The RAG service is waiting on the LLM. Either Ollama is not running (ollama serve then ollama pull thewindmom/llama3-med42-8b), or the cloud CLOUD_LLM_API_KEY is missing. Check docker compose logs rag_service for the exact error.

Database errors on startup

The database volume may be in an inconsistent state. Stop the stack, remove volumes with docker compose down -v, and restart with docker compose up --build. All migrations and seed data are re-applied automatically.

Build fails with dependency errors

Clear the Docker build cache and rebuild from scratch: docker compose build --no-cache && docker compose up. This is especially useful after pulling new commits that add Python or Node dependencies.

GDPR

This section explains how Ambience-AI-1.5 should be understood from a GDPR perspective. The project is an academic prototype, but the workflow still raises real data-protection questions because clinical prompts, uploaded files, and review notes may contain sensitive health information if used carelessly.

Data Usage Statement

Ambience-AI-1.5 processes account information, consultation records, uploaded guideline material, notifications, and audit logs in order to support the GP, specialist, and admin workflows described elsewhere in this report. In a real deployment, consultation content could amount to personal data and, in many cases, special category health data. That means data entered into the system should be treated with a higher level of care than ordinary application content.

For the purposes of this university project, the system should be presented as a prototype and demonstrations should avoid unnecessary real patient-identifying information. The academic setting does not remove GDPR concerns; it only narrows the claim being made about deployment readiness.

Key GDPR Considerations

Anyone using or evaluating the prototype should keep the following principles in mind when entering or handling data:

  • Data minimisation: only the minimum necessary clinical context should be entered into prompts, notes, or uploaded supporting material.
  • Purpose limitation: consultation data should only be used to support the defined referral and review workflow, not unrelated experimentation.
  • Access control: GP, specialist, and admin accounts should only access the information required for their role.
  • External processing risk: if a prompt or document is routed to a cloud-hosted inference provider, that creates additional processor and transfer questions that would need formal review before real deployment.
  • Auditability and retention: logs and workflow records improve traceability, but they also require clear retention and governance rules in any production setting.

This means the current project should not be described as GDPR-complete. It shows awareness of the right concerns, but it does not replace a full compliance pack, organisational policy set, or legal review.

User Responsibility

By using the system in demonstrations, testing, or handover review, users should understand that they are responsible for avoiding unnecessary personal data and for respecting the access boundaries associated with each role. The safest default for this project is to use anonymised, fictional, or otherwise non-identifying examples wherever possible.

If there is any doubt about whether information is appropriate to enter into the prototype, it should be removed or anonymised first. If there is any doubt about whether cloud routing is permissible for a dataset, the data should not be sent until the relevant legal and organisational approvals are in place.

Privacy Policy

This privacy policy explains how Ambience-AI-1.5 may use and process data when the application is used for demonstrations, evaluation, or future deployment-oriented handover. Because the platform supports clinical consultation workflows, users should assume that anything entered into the system may require a higher standard of care than ordinary consumer application content.

What data do we collect?

Ambience-AI-1.5 may collect and process data that is necessary to run its role-based clinical workflow. This can include account and authentication data, role assignments, consultation titles, specialties, severity information, message content, specialist review notes, notifications, audit-log events, and uploaded reference or supporting documents.

In a real deployment, some of this information could include personal data or special category health data if users were to enter identifiable patient information. For this reason, demonstrations and academic use should avoid unnecessary real-world identifiers wherever possible.

How do we collect your data?

You provide data to the application directly when you register an account, log in, request password-reset or verification flows, create consultations, send clinical messages, upload files, review cases, or use admin functions. Additional system data is generated automatically as part of the platform workflow, including audit logs, status transitions, notification records, and service-level operational metadata.

How will we use your data?

Ambience-AI-1.5 uses collected data to authenticate users, enforce GP, specialist, and admin permissions, manage consultation workflows, generate and revise grounded AI responses, display notifications, support specialist review, and maintain an auditable record of key actions. Operational data may also be used for debugging, reliability monitoring, and testing.

How do we store your data?

Core application data is stored within the project database layer, based on PostgreSQL and pgvector. Some transient operational data may also pass through Redis-backed queues and caches as part of notifications, retries, and background processing. If the application is configured to use an external or cloud-hosted model provider, relevant prompt content may be transmitted to that configured endpoint as part of the AI generation workflow.

The safest assumption for this project is that data should be minimised and anonymised before use. This appendix does not claim that the current prototype provides a production-ready retention, deletion, or compliance framework.

What are your data protection rights?

Under UK GDPR, users may have rights that include access to their personal data, rectification of inaccurate data, erasure in certain circumstances, restriction of processing, objection to processing, and data portability. In a real deployment, these rights would need to be supported through clear operational and legal processes, not only through interface design.

Privacy policies of other websites and services

This website contains links to external websites and services, including GitHub and other third-party resources. If a deployment uses an external inference provider or links users to external platforms, those services will have their own privacy policies and terms. This privacy policy applies only to Ambience-AI-1.5 as described in this report website.

Changes to our privacy policy

This privacy policy should be reviewed whenever the project changes its hosting model, authentication flow, storage design, or inference routing path. In particular, any move from local development to a cloud-hosted clinical context would require a more formal and complete policy review.

Retrieval and AI Dependencies

The RAG service is separated from the main backend and depends on document extraction, chunking, embedding, vector retrieval, and retry-oriented background processing. This is the part of the system that grounds responses against medical evidence rather than returning free-form outputs alone.

These dependencies support embedding generation, vector search inside PostgreSQL, PDF extraction, document chunking, Redis-backed retry workers, and routing to either local Ollama-hosted inference or a cloud Med42 endpoint.

Infrastructure and Runtime Services

Ambience-AI-1.5 is designed as a multi-service deployment rather than a single process application. The supporting runtime stack handles persistence, queueing, reverse proxying, and development-time email flows.
