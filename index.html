UCL CS Team 20 project

The Ambience-AI-1.5 Clinical Decision-Support Platform

A clinical support system that helps doctors get specialist guidance, receive evidence-grounded AI answers linked to NHS medical guidelines, and send cases for specialist review in neurology and rheumatology.

Abstract

This abstract is structured into the three required parts: problem statement, solution, and achievement and impact.

Problem statement

Clinicians dealing with neurology and rheumatology cases need answers that are both fast and traceable to credible guidance. Generic LLM chat systems can produce fluent responses, but they do not reliably expose the evidence base behind an answer and do not naturally support specialist escalation, auditability, or role-aware workflow control in a clinical consultation setting.

Our solution

Ambience-AI-1.5 implements a microservices-based consultation platform with a React frontend, a FastAPI backend, a dedicated FastAPI RAG service, PostgreSQL with pgvector, and a configurable medical LLM endpoint. A GP submits a consultation, guideline chunks are retrieved and used to generate a grounded answer with citations, and a specialist can then approve, reject, revise, or replace AI output inside the same operational workflow.

Achievement and impact

The project demonstrates an end-to-end full-stack system in which retrieval-backed generation is integrated with human clinical oversight rather than treated as a standalone chatbot feature. The implemented platform supports role-based access, consultation lifecycle tracking, citation-aware AI responses, specialist review, notifications, admin analytics, and automated testing across frontend, backend, and RAG components.
