UCL IXN Team 20 project

The Ambience-AI-1.5 Clinical Decision-Support Platform

A clinical support system that helps doctors get specialist guidance, receive evidence-grounded AI answers linked to NHS medical guidelines, and send cases for specialist review in neurology and rheumatology.

See project video Read the abstract

Abstract

Problem Statement

General Practitioners regularly ask specialists repetitive questions about monitoring thresholds, referral criteria, medication pathways, and red-flag symptoms. Finding the correct NICE and BSR guidance in long documents is time-consuming, while generic LLM chat systems may produce unsupported answers and do not provide the transparency, escalation controls, or audit trace expected in NHS-facing workflows.

Our Solution

We built a guideline-grounded RAG pipeline that ingests NICE/BSR documents, retrieves relevant passages with vector search, and generates citation-linked answers for GP queries, with low-confidence refusal and specialist escalation pathways. The architecture remains modular across frontend, backend, retrieval, and model-serving layers: after loss of access to Intel's original Gaudi 2 server environment, inference was migrated to a local model runtime with an optional cloud-hosted provider route, preserving the same safety and workflow logic.

Achievement and Impact

The delivered system demonstrates that evidence-grounded generation can be integrated into an end-to-end clinical workflow rather than exposed as a standalone chatbot. It supports role-based consultation handling, citation visibility, specialist review, and operational observability, while maintaining focus on safety targets such as evidence alignment and non-supported-claim avoidance on the project Golden Set.
